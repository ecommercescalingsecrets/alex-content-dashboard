Reverse-Engineered Playbook

The @Outscaler Content Formula

1. The Revenue Proof Post (Top Performer)

Opens with a specific dollar amount range, followed by the niche/product, then a unique insight about WHY it works.

$[Revenue Range] a month selling [Product] [X] active meta ads. [Unique insight about the niche/audience that most people miss]

2. The Myth-Buster / Contrarian

Challenges a common belief (usually "saturation") with data, then reframes the real problem.

[Bold contrarian statement] [Data point that proves it] But yeah, your problem is "[common excuse]" [Hard truth / alternative framing]

3. The Actionable Framework

Starts with "Do this" instruction, then numbered steps that deliver immediate value.

Do this for [outcome]: 1. [Step] 2. [Step] 3. [Key insight that connects the dots] 4. [The payoff / multiplier]

4. The Competitor Comparison

Names specific competing tools, then positions his product as the better alternative.

For [use case] Better than [competitor 1], [competitor 2] and [competitor 3] You need [key requirement] because of [reason] [Product name] does [specific capability] [CTA]

5. The Niche Story

Paints a picture of a niche with narrative storytelling, ending with revenue data.

[Niche] is [bold opinion] [Emotional narrative about the customer] [Why they keep buying] $[Revenue]/day selling [specific product] - [X] active meta ads

6. The Pattern/Strategy Reveal

Shares a non-obvious strategy with a real example, making the audience feel like insiders.

[Strategy observation] work really well on [platform] [Real example with link/screenshot] ([Soft CTA to tool for finding more examples])

What You Can Steal for Your Content

Always Lead with Numbers

Posts with specific $ amounts get 3-5x more engagement than generic claims. "$257K-$467K/month" beats "Making great money" every time.

50/50 Value-to-Promo Ratio

Half of posts are pure value (case studies, frameworks), half mention his product. But even promo posts lead with value first.

Attack "Saturation" Repeatedly

He hammers the same contrarian angle from different angles. Found a belief his audience holds that he disagrees with, and keeps proving it wrong with data.

Screenshots = Proof

85% of posts include images. Usually screenshots of dashboards, ad libraries, or revenue data. Visual proof > text claims.

Name Competitors Directly

Not afraid to say "Better than [tool X]". Creates instant positioning and borrows the competitor's audience through search/engagement.

Short, Punchy, Spaced Out

Almost every post uses line breaks between sentences. No walls of text. Each line is a standalone thought that keeps you scrolling.
